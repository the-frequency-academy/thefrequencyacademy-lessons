Torsion Fields — The Missing Physics

Mass creates gravity. Charge creates electromagnetism. Spin creates torsion. Every particle, atom, galaxy — and you — generates torsion fields. Soviet scientists spent decades researching this. The West largely ignored it. This lesson goes into the physics they found.

The Transmission

Physics teaches four fundamental forces. Gravity — generated by mass, bending spacetime. Electromagnetism — generated by charge, producing light and magnetism. The strong nuclear force, binding atomic nuclei. The weak nuclear force, governing radioactive decay. Four forces explain everything, we are told.

But what if spin — the angular momentum intrinsic to every particle in existence — generates its own field? Not a force like gravity that curves spacetime, but a force that twists it. A torsion. A fifth fundamental interaction, one that can transmit information instantaneously, that has handedness like a screw or a spiral galaxy, that can be stored in water and crystals and focused by geometry, and that may interact directly with consciousness.

For over fifty years, Soviet scientists pursued exactly this. More than twenty research institutes. Hundreds of papers. Billions of rubles in funding. The researchers were serious physicists — astrophysicists, mathematical physicists, biophysicists — not fringe figures. The West largely ignored the work. When the Soviet Union collapsed, much of it scattered or was classified. But the research exists. And the physics has a foundation that goes back to Einstein himself.

What Are Torsion Fields?

The word "torsion" comes from the Latin torquere — to twist. A torsion field is theorized to be a field generated by angular momentum — by spin. Every elementary particle has spin: electrons, protons, neutrons, quarks. It is an intrinsic quantum property, as fundamental as mass or charge. And just as those properties generate their respective fields, spin may generate its own.

The key insight: mass bends spacetime — that is Einstein's general relativity, curvature. Spin twists spacetime — that is torsion. A completely different geometry on the same fabric. Einstein himself recognized this and collaborated with mathematician Élie Cartan in the 1920s to develop what became known as Einstein-Cartan theory: an extension of general relativity that mathematically includes torsion alongside curvature. Mainstream physics followed the curvature branch — the part describing gravity. Soviet scientists followed the torsion branch.

In Einstein-Cartan theory, torsion is a tensor field arising from the spin density of matter. It couples to the intrinsic spin of particles rather than to their mass. In empty space, torsion propagates as a wave — a torsion wave, distinct from gravitational waves (which are curvature waves) and from electromagnetic waves (which are charge-driven oscillations). If this wave exists physically and is detectable, it constitutes a fourth type of fundamental field alongside gravity, electromagnetism, and the nuclear forces.

THE EINSTEIN-CARTAN CONNECTION

Einstein-Cartan theory is not fringe physics. It is a mathematically rigorous extension of general relativity published in peer-reviewed physics literature. The mainstream chose to work with the simpler version — curvature only, zero torsion — because for ordinary matter densities, torsion effects are predicted to be negligible. But "negligible in ordinary conditions" is not the same as "nonexistent." Soviet researchers argued that at the quantum scale and under specific conditions, torsion effects could be non-negligible and detectable.

Five Theorized Properties of Torsion Fields

Soviet and Russian researchers attributed the following properties to torsion fields, based on their experimental programs. Epistemic status: these are theorized properties with experimental support from specific research programs. They have not been verified to mainstream physics standards.

01

Instantaneous Propagation

Torsion may travel faster than light — potentially instantaneously. Nikolai Kozyrev's astronomical observations suggested that torsion signals from distant stars arrived before their light, indicating propagation velocities far exceeding c. If torsion is a twist in spacetime itself rather than a disturbance propagating through spacetime, it may not be bound by the relativistic speed limit that applies to EM radiation.

Epistemic note: Kozyrev's methodology has been contested. Superluminal propagation would require revision of special relativity or careful reframing as a near-field or vacuum effect. The strongest experimental basis for this claim remains Kozyrev's astronomical data and some Soviet apparatus results that have not been independently replicated to mainstream standards.
02

Information Without Energy

Torsion does not heat, push mechanically, or transfer energy in the conventional thermodynamic sense. It transmits patterns, signatures, and organizational information — spin-state configurations — without the energy cost of Hertzian EM transmission. This is analogous to the scalar wave property explored in Q13. The difference is that torsion has a specific proposed mechanism: it is a spin-state field, and spin-state information can be copied without energy expenditure in the same way that information can be copied without mass transfer.

Epistemic note: the information-energy relationship is genuinely subtle (see Landauer's principle). A torsion field that carries zero conventional energy while transmitting meaningful information would require careful theoretical formalization. Rein's biophysical experiments offer some empirical basis, but mechanism remains unclear.
03

Handedness — Right and Left Torsion

Torsion fields have chirality — a handedness determined by the direction of spin. Right-hand torsion (clockwise rotation viewed from above) is associated with ordering, coherence, and life-enhancing effects. Left-hand torsion (counter-clockwise) is associated with disordering and entropic effects. This mirrors the chirality found throughout biology: DNA winds right-handed, amino acids are left-handed, sugars are right-handed. If torsion interacts with biological spin systems, chirality would be the primary selectivity mechanism — left-hand torsion interacting differently with right-handed DNA than right-hand torsion does.

Epistemic note: chirality in torsion theory is mathematically well-founded in Einstein-Cartan theory, which distinguishes between the two torsion tensor orientations. The specific association with "beneficial" vs "harmful" effects is more speculative and relies on biological experiment results that haven't been widely replicated.
04

Can Be Stored in Materials

Torsion signatures can be "charged" into materials — water, crystals, metals — where the information persists after the torsion source is removed. The proposed mechanism: materials with organized atomic or molecular spin structure (water's hydrogen bond network, crystal lattices, metal conducting electron spin systems) can align their intrinsic spin states to the incoming torsion field, encoding the torsion signature as a stable spin-state configuration. This is the proposed physics behind "charged" objects in frequency work — the object's spin system holds a torsion imprint.

Epistemic note: this property requires stable spin coherence over macroscopic timescales and temperatures — something quantum decoherence generally prevents in bulk matter. The water memory debate (connected to Emoto and Benveniste) is relevant here. The property is consistent with torsion theory but lacks independent verification.
05

Interacts with Consciousness

Focused human attention may generate measurable torsion fields. Kozyrev's mirror experiments — using aluminum cylinders to focus torsion — produced conditions in which subjects reported telepathic impressions, remote viewing experiences, and altered time perception. Akimov and Shipov proposed that consciousness is a torsion phenomenon — that awareness itself is a spin-organized field pattern in the quantum vacuum, and that torsion is the physical carrier of consciousness-to-matter interaction.

Epistemic note: this is the most speculative property and the most difficult to evaluate. The Kozyrev mirror experiments are documented but involve inherently subjective measurement. The identification of consciousness with torsion fields is a philosophical as much as a scientific claim. It remains one of the most compelling and least verified aspects of the framework.

The Soviet torsion research program was substantial — not fringe science. Three key researchers defined the field across experimental, theoretical, and applied dimensions.

THE SCALE OF THE PROGRAM

Over 20 Soviet and Russian research institutes. Hundreds of peer-reviewed papers in Russian-language journals. Billions of rubles in funding during the Soviet period. Applications explored in medicine, metallurgy, agriculture, and defense. When the Soviet Union collapsed in 1991, funding dried up and many researchers dispersed — some to international academic positions, some to private industry, some simply stopped publishing. The research did not disappear. It became harder to access.

Torsion in Frequency Practice — The Missing Mechanism

Torsion theory provides proposed mechanisms for several frequency practices that lack adequate conventional explanations — situations where the effects reported exceed what standard EM can produce at the relevant intensities and distances.

Spin Direction in Coils & Rings

Winding direction in tensor rings, bifilar coils, and other copper geometries determines the chirality of the spin dynamics — and therefore the handedness of the torsion field generated.

→ Clockwise winding creates right-hand torsion (ordering). Counter-clockwise creates left-hand torsion. This is the physics basis for the instruction to wind tensor rings in a specific direction — it is not arbitrary preference but a determination of torsion polarity.

Sacred Geometry as Torsion Technology

Geometric shapes — pyramids, cones, spirals, tori — focus and direct torsion fields through phytorsion. The shape itself acts as a torsion lens, concentrating the field at specific focal points.

→ The concentration effect of pyramid geometry at the apex and one-third height point is the proposed mechanism for pyramid energy research. The torus shape continuously circulates torsion in a self-sustaining geometry. Sacred geometry is functional torsion engineering expressed in form.

Water Vortexing = Torsion Charging

Vortexing water creates angular momentum in the water — a macroscopic spin that generates torsion fields which can reorder water's hydrogen bond network through the spin-coupling mechanism.

→ This is the proposed physics behind why vortexed water shows different surface tension, crystallization, and biological activity than non-vortexed water. Oxygenation alone does not explain the magnitude of observed differences. Torsion charging provides a second mechanism operating simultaneously.

Crystal Amplification

Quartz and other crystals have highly organized atomic lattice structures with coherent spin arrangements. This coherent spin produces a coherent torsion field — and may amplify incoming torsion signals through resonant coupling.

→ The proposed mechanism for crystal enhancement of frequency devices: the crystal's coherent lattice spin provides a torsion amplification stage beyond what the device produces through EM alone. Different crystal structures would amplify different torsion signatures based on their lattice chirality.

Intention as Torsion Generation

If consciousness is a spin-organized field pattern — as Akimov and Shipov proposed — then focused intention generates a measurable torsion field. Device charging with intention is not metaphorical: it is proposed to be a physical imprinting process.

→ The Kozyrev mirror experiments, where focused human attention produced detectable effects at a distance through torsion-focusing apparatus, provide the primary experimental basis for this claim. The reproducibility of these results is the key open question.

The Ancient Names for Torsion

Chi (Chinese), prana (Sanskrit), orgone (Reich), élan vital (Bergson), od (Reichenbach) — every culture that developed a concept of life force described it as a field with properties matching torsion: directional, chirally differentiated, storable, interacting with consciousness.

→ Torsion field theory is not a new discovery that validates ancient wisdom. It is a physics framework that maps onto what ancient practitioners documented through empirical observation. The map and the territory have been developing independently and are now converging.
