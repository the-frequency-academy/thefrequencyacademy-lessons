Section 01

The Basic Definition

So what is frequency? At its most basic level, frequency is simply how many times something cycles or repeats per second.

If you tap a table once per second — that's one cycle per second. We have a name for that: one Hertz. Written as "Hz." Named after Heinrich Hertz, the physicist who first proved the existence of electromagnetic waves.

Twice per second is two Hertz. Three times, three Hertz. Simple as that.

Now let's put this in perspective with examples you already experience every day. Your heart is beating right now at somewhere between 60 and 90 times per minute — that works out to about one to one and a half Hertz. Your heart has a frequency.

The electrical grid powering your home runs at 60 Hertz in the United States — 60 cycles per second. Sound spans from about 20 Hz, a deep rumble you almost feel more than hear, all the way up to 20,000 Hz at the edge of perception.

"Frequency isn't some abstract concept. It's the pulse of everything around you. And once you start seeing the world this way, you can't unsee it."

Section 02

Frequency in Nature

The Earth has a heartbeat. It's called the Schumann Resonance, and it pulses at 7.83 Hertz — the electromagnetic frequency of the cavity between the Earth's surface and the ionosphere.

Think about that. Every living thing that has ever existed on Earth evolved within this frequency field. It's the baseline hum of our home.

Every organ in your body has an optimal frequency range. Your brain operates across different frequency bands — delta, theta, alpha, beta — each associated with different states of consciousness, from deep sleep to focused alertness. Water responds to frequency. Plants grow differently when exposed to different frequencies. Crystals have their own resonant frequencies, which is why they're used in watches and electronics.

"Nature doesn't speak in words. It speaks in frequency. And your body is designed to listen."

Section 03

Why This Matters For You

When frequencies align, we call that coherence. Coherence in your body feels like health, clarity, flow. Things work. You feel good.

When frequencies clash, we call that dissonance. Dissonance feels like stress, fatigue, dis-ease. Things struggle. You feel off.

Here's the challenge of modern life: we're surrounded by artificial frequencies. WiFi signals. Cell tower radiation. The electromagnetic fields from every device in your home. These didn't exist when your body's systems evolved. Understanding frequency gives you the power to make conscious choices about what you resonate with.

Over these 333 lessons, you'll learn not just to understand frequency — but to work with it. To build devices that generate beneficial frequencies. To structure water. To protect your space. To tune your environment and your own energy field.

"You are not solid matter. You are a symphony of frequencies. And that symphony can be tuned."
