What Is the Seed of Life?

The Seed of Life is the geometric pattern formed by seven equal circles — one central circle surrounded by six outer circles, each touching the centre and its two neighbours. It emerges directly from the Vesica Piscis construction of G2: the same compass, the same radius, the same rule. You simply continue.

What makes the Seed extraordinary is its inevitability. Six circles always fit. Not approximately — exactly. No gap, no overlap, no adjustment required. This is guaranteed by the geometry of the equilateral triangle and the hexagon. Six times sixty degrees is three hundred and sixty degrees: a complete circle. The circle's own mathematics ensure the fit is perfect.

The Seed is the generative DNA of the entire sacred geometry tradition — every lesson in this module that follows traces back to these seven circles.

  • Seven equal circles: one central, six surrounding, each touching the centre and its two neighbours
  • Constructed by continuing the Vesica Piscis — same radius, compass at each new intersection point
  • Six fits one exactly because 6 × 60° = 360° — a geometric inevitability, not a coincidence
  • Seven-fold symbolism: days of creation, chakras, musical notes, colours, planets, days of the week
  • Generates the Egg of Life, the Flower of Life, Metatron's Cube, and all five Platonic Solids
  • Six-fold symmetry appears throughout nature: snowflakes, honeycombs, crystal lattice, quartz

From Vesica to Seed — The Construction

The Seed of Life emerges when the Vesica Piscis construction is continued — adding one new circle at each intersection point until seven circles form the complete template.

  • 1

    Start with Your Vesica Piscis

    Begin with your completed G2 construction: two circles of equal radius, centre points A and B, and intersection points C and D. This is your foundation.

  • 2

    Place the Third Circle

    Place the compass needle at intersection point C. Draw a third circle with the same radius. New intersection points appear where this circle meets the first two.

  • 3

    Continue at Each New Intersection

    Continue placing circles at each new intersection point, always the same radius, always moving around the central circle. Each new circle creates new intersections for the next.

  • 4

    The Sixth Outer Circle Closes

    After six outer circles, the ring closes. The sixth circle's edge meets the first outer circle's edge exactly — no gap, no gap adjustment needed. The pattern locks.

  • 5

    The Seed Is Complete

    Seven circles total: one central, six surrounding. Each outer circle touches the central circle and its two neighbours. You have drawn the Seed of Life — the template from which all else emerges.

Why Six Fits One — The Geometric Proof

This is one of the most elegant facts in geometry. Why does exactly six circles fit around one central circle of the same radius? Not five, not seven — exactly six?

6 equilateral triangles × 60° = 360°
Six equilateral triangles tile a hexagon perfectly — leaving no gap and no overlap. This is not approximation. It is a geometric inevitability.

When two circles of the same radius overlap so that each passes through the other's centre, they form an equilateral triangle (G2). Six such triangles — each with a 60° angle at the shared centre — fill the full 360° around that point. You cannot make seven fit. You cannot make five fit. Six is the only number the geometry permits.

Quick Reference — Key Definitions

Seed of Life ›
The geometric pattern of seven equal circles — one central circle surrounded by six outer circles, each touching the centre and its two neighbours. The foundational generative template of sacred geometry.
Six-fold Symmetry ›
Rotational symmetry where a form looks identical after 60° rotation (360÷6). The Seed has perfect six-fold symmetry — found in snowflakes, honeycombs, carbon crystal lattice, and quartz. This symmetry is a consequence of the equilateral triangle's 60° angles.
Egg of Life ›
The three-dimensional equivalent of the Seed of Life — seven spheres in 3D space. Mirrors the geometric stages of human embryonic cell division (2, 4, 8 cells). The Egg of Life is the bridge between the 2D Seed and the 3D forms of the Platonic Solids.
Diatonic Scale ›
The seven-note musical scale (Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Ti) — the sonic expression of the Seed of Life's seven-fold template, the foundation of Western music. Each note corresponds to one circle in the Seed.
Genesis Template ›
The interpretation of the Seed as the geometric blueprint of the six days of creation in Genesis — central circle as the void/potential, each outer circle as one day. The completion of the six days corresponds to the closing of the six-circle ring.
Vesica Progression ›
The process of adding equal circles at each new intersection point of the Vesica Piscis construction until the Seed emerges. Each circle creates new intersections, which become centres for subsequent circles. This same progression continues to generate the Flower of Life.
