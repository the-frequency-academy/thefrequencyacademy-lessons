What Is the Torus?

A torus is the surface generated by rotating a circle around an axis that is in the same plane as the circle but does not intersect it. The result is the surface of a doughnut. Mathematically, it is defined by two radii: the major radius R (distance from the centre of the torus to the centre of the tube) and the minor radius r (the radius of the tube itself).

The torus is topologically distinct from a sphere. A sphere has genus 0 — no holes. A torus has genus 1 — one hole through the middle. In topology, a coffee cup and a doughnut are the same shape (both genus 1). No amount of continuous stretching, bending, or deforming can turn a torus into a sphere or vice versa. They are fundamentally different kinds of surface.

The torus is the simplest closed surface with non-trivial topology — the simplest shape where inside and outside are not straightforwardly separable. It is also the fundamental geometry of every self-sustaining energy system in the universe.

  • Generated by rotating a circle around an external axis — two radii (R, r) fully define the geometry
  • Topologically genus 1 (one hole) — fundamentally distinct from the sphere (genus 0)
  • Self-referencing: energy flows through the axis, arcs outward, returns to re-enter — output becomes input
  • Found at every scale: proton fields, water molecules, the human heart, the human biofield, Earth magnetosphere, heliosphere, galactic field
  • Prerequisite for understanding Merkaba (G15–16), tensor rings (G20), and all field-based energy work

Quick Reference — Key Definitions

Torus ›
A surface generated by rotating a circle around an axis in the same plane but not intersecting it. The shape of a doughnut. The fundamental geometry of every self-sustaining electromagnetic and energy field in the universe. Defined by two radii: major (R) and minor (r).
Major Radius (R) ›
The distance from the centre of a torus to the centre of its tube. Defines the overall size of the toroidal form. A larger R creates a wider, more open torus. Together with the minor radius r, R fully defines the geometry of the toroidal surface.
Minor Radius (r) ›
The radius of the tube of a torus. Defines the thickness of the toroidal ring. Together with the major radius R, it fully defines the geometry of the toroidal surface. When r approaches R, the central hole closes; when r is much smaller than R, the torus becomes thin and ring-like.
Topology ›
The branch of mathematics concerned with properties of space that are preserved under continuous deformations (stretching, bending, twisting — but not tearing or gluing). The torus (genus 1, one hole) is topologically distinct from the sphere (genus 0, no holes). A coffee cup and a doughnut are topologically identical — both genus 1.
Self-Referencing Field ›
A field whose output re-enters as input, sustaining itself through continuous flow. The toroidal field is perfectly self-referencing: energy flows through the central axis, arcs around the outside, and returns to re-enter the centre. The field feeds itself — a self-sustaining system in dynamic equilibrium.
Magnetosphere ›
The region around a planet dominated by its magnetic field. Earth's magnetosphere is toroidal in geometry, shaped by the interaction of the planetary magnetic dipole with the solar wind. The Van Allen radiation belts follow the toroidal structure of the magnetosphere, trapping charged particles in two belt-shaped regions.
