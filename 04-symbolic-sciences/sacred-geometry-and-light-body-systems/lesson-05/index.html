What Is Metatron's Cube?

Metatron's Cube is the sacred geometry figure produced by connecting the centres of all 13 Fruit of Life circles with straight lines. It is derived from the Flower of Life in exactly two steps, and within its network of 78 lines, all five Platonic Solids are simultaneously present and extractable.

It is the most information-dense single geometric form in sacred geometry — containing within one 2D figure the geometric record of every possible three-dimensional form. This is not metaphor. Each of the five Platonic Solids can be physically traced within the lines of the Cube by selecting specific subsets.

Key insight: 13 centres, every connection drawn once: 12 × 13 ÷ 2 = 78 lines. The triangular number of 12. All five Platonic Solids encoded simultaneously within those 78 lines.

From Flower to Fruit to Cube — Two Steps

Step 1 — The Fruit of Life

Within the 19 circles of the Flower of Life, select 13 using a specific skip pattern: the central circle plus 12 others taken at every other intersection through the outer rings. These 13 circles are the Fruit of Life.

If the Flower is all curves and organic flow — yin — the Fruit is its yang complement: the hidden skeleton, the structure within the curves. The 13 circles map precisely to the 10 Sephiroth of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life plus the 3 hidden spheres, the complete map of divine emanation in the Western mystery tradition.

13 is a Fibonacci number. 13 is the number of transformation. 13 is the number of the Christ Consciousness grid. And 13 is exactly how many circles the Fruit of Life contains.

Step 2 — Connect All Centres

Now draw a straight line from every one of the 13 Fruit centres to every other centre. Every single possible connection — not selecting, not filtering. All of them.

12 × 13 ÷ 2 = 78 lines
The triangular number of 12. The exact count when every centre connects to every other exactly once.

That figure — 13 circles, 78 lines — is Metatron's Cube. And within it, all five Platonic Solids are simultaneously visible.

Archangel Metatron & the Kabbalistic Tradition

In Kabbalistic and Enochian tradition, Metatron is the archangel who records divine thought in geometric form. He is called the Scribe of God and the Chancellor of Heaven. The Cube is described as his primary tool — the instrument through which the divine architect organises the structure of creation.

The 13 circles of the Fruit of Life correspond to the 10 Sephiroth of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life plus the 3 hidden spheres — the Da'at, and the two aspects of Kether — completing the full map of divine emanation in the Western mystery tradition. This is not incidental correspondence: the geometry maps to the theology with exact precision.

Whether or not you work within the Kabbalistic tradition, the geometry makes a statement that stands on its own: this figure contains all possible three-dimensional form. Whatever tradition first encoded it, they understood the structure of space completely.

The five Platonic Solids appear throughout the natural and scientific world: in Plato's cosmology, in Kepler's early model of the solar system, in carbon molecule geometry (buckminsterfullerene is an icosahedron derivative), in virus capsid architecture, and in the proposed geometry of the cosmos itself.

Quick Reference — Key Definitions

Metatron's Cube ›
The sacred geometry figure produced by connecting the centres of all 13 Fruit of Life circles with straight lines. Contains all five Platonic Solids simultaneously. The most information-dense single geometric form in sacred geometry.
Fruit of Life ›
The 13-circle pattern selected from within the Flower of Life using a specific skip pattern. The template from which Metatron's Cube is derived by connecting all centres. 13 is a Fibonacci number and corresponds to the Kabbalistic Tree of Life plus hidden spheres.
Platonic Solid ›
One of five 3D forms in which every face is an identical regular polygon and every vertex is surrounded by the same number of faces. The only five such forms possible in 3D space: tetrahedron, hexahedron, octahedron, dodecahedron, icosahedron.
Archangel Metatron ›
In Kabbalistic and Enochian tradition, the archangel who records divine thought in geometric form. Called the Scribe of God and Chancellor of Heaven. The Cube is described as his instrument for organising the structure of creation.
Euler's Formula ›
For any convex polyhedron: Vertices minus Edges plus Faces = 2. Holds for all five Platonic Solids and confirms their geometric integrity. A fundamental theorem of topology that connects these forms across mathematics.
Duality ›
Two Platonic Solids are dual when the vertices of one correspond to the face centres of the other. Cube and Octahedron are duals. Dodecahedron and Icosahedron are duals. The Tetrahedron is self-dual — its dual is another tetrahedron.
