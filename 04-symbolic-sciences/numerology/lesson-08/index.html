Architectural proportions are not merely aesthetic preferences. They are vibrational decisions. The dimensions of the space determine exactly where those nodes and antinodes fall. Sacred architecture deliberately positioned those patterns at the places where people sat, stood, prayed, or made decisions.

03
Four Principles for Your Own Sacred Space

You don't need a pyramid. You need an intentional space — even a corner of a room — designed with numerical awareness. These four principles are extracted directly from the architectural traditions above.

PRINCIPLE 1 — PROPORTIONS

The most powerful single change you can make to any space: set its primary surface (altar, desk, meditation mat area) to phi proportions. Length ÷ width = 1.618. This embeds the growth ratio of living nature into the geometry of your space. You do not need to be precise — approximate phi is sufficient. 63 × 39 cm, 89 × 55 cm, 144 × 89 cm — all Fibonacci pairs approximating phi.

PRINCIPLE 2 — OBJECT COUNT

Count your objects intentionally. Three candles (creation). Seven crystals (spiritual structure). Nine stones (completion). Twelve items on an altar (complete systemic organisation). The number of objects in your space is a frequency decision. Everything counted has a digit sum. Choose it with the same care you'd choose the frequency of a device.

PRINCIPLE 3 — CARDINAL ALIGNMENT

Face your space toward a cardinal direction or its sacred numerological equivalent. North = 8 (power, completion of cycle). East = 1 (new beginnings, initiation — where the sun rises). South = 3 (creative expression, outward movement). West = 6 (love, completion, the sun setting into the heart). Align your altar, meditation seat, or workspace accordingly.

PRINCIPLE 4 — PERSONAL NUMBERS

Encode your own numbers into the space. Set the number of dominant objects to your Life Path number. Choose altar dimensions whose digit sums align with your Expression number. Place items in groupings that reflect your current Personal Year. The space becomes a physical expression of your numerological identity — a mirror of your frequency in material form.
